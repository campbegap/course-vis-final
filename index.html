School of Applied & Creative Computing

At Purdue Polytechnic’s School of Applied and Creative Computing, students explore the intersection of computing, design, and innovation through a dynamic, interdisciplinary approach. The school emphasizes hands-on learning, applied research, and real-world impact, blending technical knowledge with creative thinking. With a focus on advancing both the science and the art of computing, students are prepared to become adaptable problem-solvers and forward-thinking leaders in an ever-evolving digital world. The School of Applied and Creative Computing provides solutions to emerging industry needs in a rapidly-evolving computing landscape.

Students gain hands-on experience in areas such as cybersecurity, data analytics, web development, animation, and user experience design. These programs prepare graduates for careers in fast-growing industries.

About Purdue University

History

Purdue University was founded in 1869 as a land-grant school focused on science, engineering, and agriculture. It was named after John Purdue, who helped fund the university. Today, Purdue has major campuses in West Lafayette and Indianapolis, and has grown into a leading research school known for hands-on learning and innovation, especially in technology and engineering.

Purdue is known for its strong engineering and technology programs, and has produced many notable alumni, including Neil Armstrong, Orville Redenbacher, and Drew Brees.

Fun Facts

  • Neil Armstrong, the first person to land on the moon, attended Purdue
  • The school colors are Gold and Black
  • Purdue has over 200 undergraduate majors
  • The Boilermaker Special is the official mascot
